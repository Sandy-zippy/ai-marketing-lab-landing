# AI Marketing Labs: Go-To-Market + Operations Blueprint

Scorecard funnel, founding cohort, 1-on-1 DFY. Solo operator + AI agents, lean budget, India-first + US/UK/CA/Gulf. Method: Priestley (scorecard, waitlist, oversubscribed, value ladder).

## Start here: the 20% that matters

1. **Deploy the Apps Script today.** Alert + auto-responder are built. Instant first-touch on every lead is the cheapest conversion lift available.
2. **Wire the comment-keyword to DM to quiz bridge (ManyChat).** The content engine exists; this is the missing pipe between attention and the funnel.
3. **Make the 6 thank-you build plans feel unmistakably personal.** The entire Priestley mechanic rests on "this diagnosed ME." This page is the funnel's hinge; iterate it with Clarity recordings weekly.
4. **Ship the cohort anchor asset + honest waitlist mechanics page.** Nobody joins a waitlist for a vague future thing; they join for a specific, capped, priced-for-founders thing with a clear "dates come here first" promise.
5. **Publish 3 to 4 reels/week from the existing 40-script bank, re-pointed at the quiz.** Consistency over cleverness; the scripts are already written.
6. **One weekly email to the list, from week 1, even at 20 subscribers.** The waitlist is only warm if it hears from you weekly. The list is the launch.
7. **Track one funnel view weekly (PostHog: land to complete to join, by source) and fix exactly one leak per week.** One improvement/week compounds; five half-fixes do not.
8. **Do not spend on cold ads until the organic quiz to waitlist to paid math is known.** Retargeting pixel now, cold budget after cohort 1 proves the economics.

## 1. Funnel map, end to end

The spine: every stage feeds the next a warmer, better-segmented person. The scorecard result is the segmentation key that must travel with the lead through everything (Sheet column, email tag, WhatsApp field, PostHog property).

## 3. Messaging system

**Big idea:** "Stop chatting with AI. Put agents on payroll." You do not need 50 tools or prompts. You need a small fleet of AI marketing agents that do the work while you sleep, and you can build the first one this week. Sandy runs his own marketing on the exact 6-agent fleet he teaches (Claude Code, n8n, Composio, Mem0) and builds it in public.

**Message pillars:**
1. **Agents, not chats.** ChatGPT gives you drafts; agents give you outcomes. The gap between AI-curious and AI-operated is a system, not a subscription.
2. **Proof in public.** Every claim is a screen recording of the actual fleet running. No fabricated numbers, ever; show the build instead.
3. **One agent first, then the fleet.** Overwhelm is the enemy. The scorecard tells you which ONE agent to build first; the cohort takes you from one to six in six weeks.
4. **Operator to operator.** Built by a working marketer in Hyderabad for marketers, founders, and agency owners who ship. Real stack, real builds, small cohort.

**Objection map:**

| Objection | Counter |
|---|---|
| "I'm not technical / Claude Code sounds like coding" | Every cohort build is live, step-by-step, screen-shared. If you can follow a recipe you can follow a build. Week-0 setup sprint gets your environment working before week 1 |
| "I already use ChatGPT" | That is exactly what the scorecard diagnoses. Chat is labor; agents are leverage |
| "Another AI course?" | Not a course: 6 live builds, you leave with 6 working agents wired to your own accounts. Deliverable is software, not certificates |
| "Price / is it worth it?" | Waitlist-first pricing rewards early commitment. Anchor against one freelancer month or one tool stack's annual bill. Never fabricate ROI |
| "Will this work with MY business?" | The scorecard already personalized your start. Builds are templated but wired to your stack; 1-on-1 exists if you want it fully done |
| "Why now? There's no date" | Dates go to the waitlist first, seats capped at 100 with 37 taken, founding pricing dies with cohort 1. Joining costs nothing and locks the option |
| "Can't I learn this free on YouTube?" | Yes, in 6 scattered months. The cohort is compression: sequence, templates, live debugging, a group shipping alongside you |

**By stage/temperature:**
- **Cold (awareness):** Pillars 1 and 2 only. Curiosity + proof. Never mention the cohort; CTA is always the quiz.
- **Warm (quiz takers):** Pillar 3. Speak to their scorecard segment by name. Objection-handling lives here.
- **Hot (waitlist, cart open):** Pillar 4 + scarcity. Seat counts, founding pricing, member builds. Goes ONLY to the waitlist; scarcity claims must be literally true.
- **1-on-1 track:** operator framing: "you want the system, not the skill; we build it, you own it." Triggered by segment + behavior.

## 4. Creative system

The engine already exists: 40 shoot-ready scripts + voice skill + reel-factory (Remotion) + carousel-factory + AIML style rules (monochrome, no-lime, Pixelify hooks-only). The job is to point it at this funnel.

**Formats and cadence (solo-operator sustainable):**

| Format | Cadence | Purpose |
|---|---|---|
| Talking-head reel (from bank, re-CTA'd to quiz) | 3/wk | Awareness pillars 1 and 2 |
| Build-in-public screen-recording reel | 1/wk | Proof; doubles as cohort demo |
| Carousel (agent teardown / stack listicle) | 1/wk | Saves + shares; LinkedIn repurpose |
| LinkedIn text post (repurposed script) | 2 to 3/wk | Founder/agency ICP |
| Weekly operator email | 1/wk | Nurture + waitlist warmth |
| Story sequence (poll) | 2 to 3/wk | Cheap engagement, quiz link in sticker |

Batch model: one shoot day/week covers all reels; agents handle edit, captions, carousels. That is itself content.

**Content-to-funnel loop:** reel hook maps to one of the 6 agents, comment keyword, ManyChat DM with quiz link (UTM = reel slug), quiz result, segment-tagged nurture that references the SAME agent the reel hooked them with. PostHog closes the loop: which reel slug produced quiz completions, not just views.

**Scorecard result seeds content:** each outcome becomes a franchise ("You should build the Follow-up Agent first, here's the 15-minute version") as a reel, carousel, email, and nurture asset. 6 agents x 4 formats = a 24-asset matrix that fills a month. Quiz answer distribution tells you which agent the market wants; make more of that one.

**Must-exist assets for launch:** (1) six distinct thank-you build plans (live, verify each reads personal), (2) segment nurture sequences or at minimum a segment-specific first email, (3) a "what the cohort is" anchor asset (60-90s reel + a site section: curriculum, 6 builds, seat structure, waitlist mechanics), (4) 3 launch reels re-cut with the quiz CTA, (5) waitlist confirmation email + WhatsApp message, (6) 1-on-1 qualification form + a "how DFY works" section, (7) 2 retargeting statics held until pixel is live.

## 6. Launch plan

**Phase 0: wire and seed (weeks 1-2).** Ship ops items 1-5. Publish the cohort anchor section + waitlist mechanics. Start the content cadence with quiz CTAs. Personal-network seeding: Sandy DMs his warm circle the quiz directly. Target: first 50-100 quiz completions to validate the funnel math and each thank-you page. Review Clarity + PostHog after the first 100 sessions; fix the single biggest quiz leak.

**Phase 1: soft launch, waitlist build (weeks 3-6).** Full organic cadence + ManyChat funnel. 1 community/partnership play per week. Nurture sequences live (email first; WhatsApp as wired). Collect cohort-intent signal (one email asks "what would you build first?", replies = hot list). Turn on retargeting at modest spend once the pixel has an audience. Gate to Phase 2: waitlist at roughly 5x founding seats (about 150-200) OR 8 weeks elapsed. If 8 weeks pass under target, announce cohort 1 to whatever list exists at a smaller seat count, and keep the public seat number honest, never faked.

**Phase 2: cohort 1 launch (2-week open window).** Announce dates to the waitlist ONLY (email + WhatsApp), 48h early-access founding price, then general waitlist access. Daily launch content during open cart: member spotlights (with consent), build previews, honest seat counts. Close hard on the stated date. Oversubscribed mechanics only work if the close is real. 1-on-1 pitched to waitlist non-buyers who fit the DFY profile after cart close.

**Phase 3: deliver + compound (weeks 7-13).** Cohort delivery becomes the content: every weekly live build yields 2-3 reels and one email. Capture testimonials at weeks 2, 4, 6. Week 6 ritual: referral ask + DFY upsell + cohort 2 waitlist opens to the public with cohort 1 proof. Only now evaluate cold paid, per the launch-strategy skill's event-volume gates.

**Weekly cadence (solo operator):** Mon = metrics review + plan; Tue = shoot day (batch reels); Wed = agents edit/render, write weekly email; Thu = publish + community + LinkedIn; Fri = nurture/DM replies, 1-on-1 calls, one funnel improvement shipped; Daily (15 min) = comments and DMs.

**KPIs (starting hypotheses to calibrate, not benchmarks to claim):**
1. Quiz landings/week by source: growing WoW; target 150+ by end of Phase 1.
2. Quiz completion (start to submitted): 40-60%. Below 30% = fix the capture step.
3. Thank-you to waitlist join: 25-40% (warm/organic).
4. Nurture engagement: email open 40%+; reply rate is the real signal.
5. Waitlist size vs seats: 5-10x founding seats before dates announced.
6. Waitlist to paid at launch: 10-25% for a warm, scarcity-run list. Below 10% = pricing or trust gap.
7. 1-on-1: qualified inquiries/month and inquiry to call rate.
8. Content-to-funnel efficiency: quiz completions per reel published (views are diagnostic, not a goal).

**Key assumptions:** GHL WABA as interim WhatsApp sender (revisit at TwentyCRM Phase 2); Sheet + Beehiiv tags as CRM until volume justifies more; 37/100 seat structure treated as fixed. All target ranges are calibration hypotheses, not promised outcomes.
